## Strategy Direction: Commodity-Linked Cross-Sectional FX (Direction 1 Expansion)

Building on the Direction 1 cross-sectional FX spine, a high-conviction non-duplicate direction is the **Commodity-Currency Divergence**. This strategy ranks the "commodity trio" (AUD, NZD, CAD) against their underlying primary export indices (Iron Ore, Dairy, Crude Oil) rather than against other currencies.

**Thesis:** Currency markets often lag sharp moves in commodity price indices due to multi-month corporate hedging contracts. When the AUD/CAD cross-sectional rank significantly diverges from the Iron-Ore/Oil relative price ratio, a reversion entry is triggered once the commodity trend stabilizes. This fits the Edge Lab charter: it has a structural cause (commercial hedging lag), a mechanical price signature (ratio divergence), is news-blackout safe (daily rebalance), and maintains a market-neutral profile by trading the commodity currency spread, making it highly FTMO-compatible.
