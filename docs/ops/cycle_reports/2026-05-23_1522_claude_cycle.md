### Root causes

Two distinct generations of card failure:

**Generation 1 (early cards):** Have R1–R4 `PASS` but were written in a mixed German/English
format. The `filters` regex (`(?m)^#{1,6}\s+filters?\b`) requires the English word
"filters" as a heading; German cards use "Filter" which likely case-matches but the
section structure differs. The `thesis`, `falsification`, `q08_q11_risks`, and
`implementation_notes` sections are absent or use different headings. Affects ~88–98% of cards.

**Generation 2 (later batch):** Have R1–R4 set to `UNKNOWN` — the G0 review pass that
wrote these cards used a different status vocabulary, not yet upgraded to explicit PASS.
Affects ~44–49% of cards.

Both generations have accumulated simultaneously, blocking 100% of the library.

### Remediation options (for OWNER decision)

**Option A — Batch card enrichment (Codex task):** For each blocked card, auto-add
the missing required sections and upgrade `UNKNOWN` → `PASS`/`FAIL` where the body
content supports it. High-confidence cases for UNKNOWN → PASS: cards where the body
clearly meets the R1–R4 criteria but the frontmatter field was not explicitly set.
Estimated effort: 1–2 Codex tasks, ~1–2 hours runtime.

**Option B — Schema relaxation:** Add German section headings to the `filters` regex,
relax the `thesis`/`falsification` patterns to catch common alternate section names.
Risk: loosens the gate that ensures cards are complete before building.

**Option C — Cards_review enrichment first:** The 34 cards in `cards_review` are
presumably written to the current schema. Approve those and use them as the initial
build queue while the 2129-card remediation runs in parallel.

Recommendation: **Option A + Option C in parallel.** The `cards_review` queue gives
immediate throughput; the batch enrichment restores the full library.
