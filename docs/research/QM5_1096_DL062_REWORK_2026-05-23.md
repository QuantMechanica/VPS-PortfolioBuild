## 4. Diagnosis

Unlike QM5_1044/1048/1088/1089 in this DL-062 wave, this is **not** a basket-EA
mismatch. The Donchian breakout is per-chart by construction; the harness shape is
correct. Three real defects:

1. **Dispatcher fanout (primary noise source)** — 31 of 37 rows ran on FX/UK100/XNGUSD/
   SP500.DWX — none of which the card targets. Per
   [[project_qm_dispatcher_universe_mismatch_2026-05-23]]: the work_items dispatcher
   ignores `card.target_symbols` and fans the EA across the full DWX universe. Those
   31 rows are routing noise inflating the 0.944 zero-trade pct.
2. **`min_trades_required` ratcheted up between waves** — went from 5 (in-universe
   2026-05-17) to 24 (out-of-universe 2026-05-18). A 24-trade/yr floor on a D1
   Donchian-20 channel is dimensionally wrong: the textbook turtle-style channel only
   fires when price breaks a 20-day extreme, which on trending instruments produces
   2-10 trades/yr per symbol, not 24. Whoever raised the gate didn't read the strategy
   class.
3. **Card `expected_trades_per_year_per_symbol: 50` is itself overstated** — Donchian-20
   D1 with opposite-channel exit and ATR stop on trend-friendly metals/indices
   historically produces 5-15 trades/yr/symbol, depending on volatility regime. 50/yr
   would require fast oscillation between channels, which the strategy explicitly
   avoids (it's a *trend-following* sleeve, not a range-mean-reversion sleeve). This
   miscalibration upstream is what propagated into a too-high min_trades floor.

But — and this is the difference from the basket cases — **the 4 in-universe
MIN_TRADES_NOT_MET rows at min_trades=5 are genuine evidence**: even at a charitable
5-trade floor, the EA didn't fire 5 D1-Donchian breakouts on NDX/XAUUSD/XTIUSD/GDAXI
in year 2024. That deserves a second look at the implementation (was `shift=1` applied
correctly? Did the volatility-floor filter `ATR/Close < 0.004` swallow most signals on
quiet 2024 NDX?) — but it is *not* the kind of evidence that justifies relaxing the
breakout rule. The Donchian rule is the entire thesis; relaxing it dissolves the
strategy.

## 5. Recommended change vector

**DO NOT relax entry conditions or substitute signal logic.** The Donchian-20 channel
*is* the strategy class; "relaxing the entry" turns it into a different paper.

Sequenced rework path (no card rewrite needed for steps 1–3):

1. **Enforce the dispatcher whitelist** ([[project_qm_dispatcher_universe_mismatch_2026-05-23]])
   so QM5_1096 re-runs only against XAU/XAG/XTI/NDX/WS30/GDAXI. This alone collapses 31
   of the 37 noise rows from the next cycle.
2. **Re-calibrate `min_trades_required`** for this strategy class: ~5–8 trades/yr/symbol
   floor for D1 Donchian-20, not 24. The gate config that picked 24 needs to read the
   card's `expected_trades_per_year_per_symbol` rather than apply a universal floor.
3. **Re-run the 6 in-universe symbols** with the correct gate. If trade counts still
   miss the recalibrated floor on more than half the universe, *then* the strategy
   signal (not the harness) is the suspect, and the next step becomes either:
   - widen the test window beyond a single year (Donchian on quiet years systematically
     under-trades; needs a 2017–2024 span to fairly judge), or
   - lift the `ATR/Close < 0.004` volatility floor to verify it isn't suppressing valid
     entries in 2024's NDX low-vol regime (single-parameter inspection only — not a
     thesis rewrite).
4. **Correct the card**: drop `expected_trades_per_year_per_symbol` from 50 to a
   defensible 8–12 with a one-line annotation in `Lessons Learned`. This is a card
   metadata fix, not a strategy change.

Only if steps 1–4 still produce widespread MIN_TRADES_NOT_MET across a 2017–2024
in-universe window does this become a real RECYCLE / DEAD candidate. Per
[[project_qm_p2_backtest_policy_2026-05-22]]: do not change Model=4 or shorten the
window as a workaround.

## 6. Q08 / Q11 risks (carry-forward)

- Q08: pure trend-following with no regime filter — whipsaw tail in the Donchian
  literature is well-known. The opposite-channel exit + ATR stop bounds single-trade
  loss, but the *cumulative* whipsaw drawdown in choppy years (2018, mid-2023 metals)
  needs explicit DD-curve evidence before FTMO promotion (≤10% total DD binds).
- Q11: low-frequency trend EA on a 6-symbol universe with a 1-year backtest window has
  ~30–60 decisions total. Per-symbol-per-year P3 cells will be sparse; need either
  a multi-year stitched view or a basket-aggregated reporting layer to make the
  pipeline numbers honest.
