## Defect Analysis

### Defect 1: Dispatcher Universe Mismatch

The card declares 4 target symbols. The dispatcher enqueued ~37 DWX symbols. For 29
of them no set file exists → `INVALID / setfile_missing`. This is the same recurring
dispatcher defect seen in QM5_1088, QM5_1089, QM5_1048, QM5_10020.

These 29 symbols consume MT5 slots and inflate DL-062 failure counts without any signal
content.

**Action required:** OPS_FIX to enforce card.target_symbols at enqueue (tracking as
recurring class).

### Defect 2: Zero / Near-Zero Trades on Even Target Symbols

EURUSD.DWX is a target symbol with a set file. It ran twice and both times produced
`INVALID_REPORT;INCOMPLETE_RUNS`. This is a zero-trade or near-zero-trade signal from
the real H4 backtest on a primary target symbol.

The NNFX V2 entry stack is very restrictive:

**H4 regime (must be true simultaneously):**
- Close > EMA(100) — broad uptrend
- MACD main > signal — momentum confirming
- SSL(10) green > red — short-term bias confirming
- All three must flip together to give a bias

**H1 compression filter (before entry):**
- H1 ATR(14) must be below its 80-bar median during ≥3 of the prior 8 closed H1 bars
  (i.e., volatility must have recently contracted for at least 3 hours)

**H1 entry (when bias + compression are met):**
- Close above 20-bar H1 Donchian channel high (breakout)
- RSI(14) H1 ≥ 52 for longs (or ≤ 48 for shorts)
- No position; not in last 2 H1 bars before weekly close

This four-layer stack (H4 triple confirmation + H1 compression + Donchian breakout +
RSI) imposes compound probability on entry. Each layer is individually reasonable but
their product is the source of the zero-trade problem: EURUSD in 2022 (the smoke year
period) was in a strong USD-driven downtrend — the H4 long-bias conditions (EMA100 +
MACD + SSL simultaneously bullish) would rarely all be green during this regime.

**Key vulnerability:** The strategy is designed for trending markets where the H4
triple-stack fires cleanly. In choppy or counter-trend regimes (e.g., EURUSD 2022
Fed hiking cycle), the bias layer may never synchronize all three indicators.

**Expected trades per year per symbol declared: 70.** The smoke year evidence suggests
the actual frequency is well below this — the 80-bar compression filter (≈ 80 H1 bars
= 3.3 days of lookback) combined with the Donchian-20 breakout after that compression
requires a very specific volatility contraction → expansion sequence that may occur
far fewer than 70 times per year across 4 symbols.

## Verdict

**Two-part fix required:**

### Part A — OPS_FIX (immediate, infra)
Cancel all non-target work items (29 symbols with setfile_missing). Re-enqueue only the
4 declared target symbols: EURUSD.DWX, GBPUSD.DWX, USDJPY.DWX, XAUUSD.DWX.

### Part B — Card Signal Rework (strategy quality)
The EURUSD zero-trade result on a target symbol is a genuine signal-quality issue.
Recommended rework options (pick one, not all):

**Option 1 — Relax H4 compression filter:**  
Lower the compression threshold from "80-bar median for ≥3 of 8 bars" to "80-bar median
for ≥1 of 8 bars" (at least one recent H1 bar contracted). This widens the entry window
while preserving the directional compression-expansion thesis.

**Option 2 — Relax H4 triple-stack to 2-of-3:**  
Require only 2 of {EMA100, MACD, SSL} to confirm the H4 bias. This broadens the regime
filter from strict triple-agreement to supermajority, increasing participation in
partial-trend environments.

**Option 3 — Add a broader time-frame filter replacement:**  
Replace the 3-indicator H4 stack with a single reliable trend filter (e.g., H4 close
above/below the 50-period H4 EMA alone), reducing compound dependency.

Recommended option: **Option 2** — relaxing to 2-of-3 preserves the NNFX multi-factor
philosophy while allowing entries when one indicator lags. This is the smallest change
to the strategy's core structure.

**Expected outcome after rework:** 20–40 trades/year/symbol on EURUSD (vs. declared
70) — the Donchian breakout after compression is inherently a lower-frequency signal
than the card estimates.
